# INTRO — Chapter 1 summary

Format mirrors js/phases/intro.js: each block is one step.
Style: title sentence case, description = imperative + period, no em-dashes / colons / semicolons.


STEP 1 — Accept how dating works
Description: Prepare before dating. Don't wing it.
Tips:
- Dating goes meet, date, get exclusive, then maintain it for the long run.
- Know your dealbreakers and red lines before you start.
- Romantic feelings are conditional. Your behaviour moves the needle up or down.
- The spark sits on a thin line. Once it drops below, it doesn't come back.
- If someone rejects you over height, cup size or some other trivial trait, you've dodged a bullet.
- Personality beats superficial traits almost every time.


STEP 2 — Sort your appearance
Description: Present the best version of yourself.
Tips:
- Dress nice in simple, good quality clothes that fit and feel comfortable.
- Smell nice and look clean.
- Shave or trim head, face and body hair to your taste.
- Cut and clean your nails.
- Avoid worn out clothes.


STEP 3 — Build your confidence
Description: Fake it till you make it.
Tips:
- Everyone has insecurities. Confident people are the only ones in the room who know about theirs.
- Don't be an emotionless brick. Be vulnerable later, once trust is there.
- No self-deprecating humour. It undermines the confidence you're trying to project.
- Slow yourself down. Don't flail around.
- Keep a relaxed posture, straighten up, shoulders back.
- Practice maintaining eye contact with people you interact with.
- Expect the first few hundred attempts to feel awkward. It gets easier.
- Ignore charisma gurus. They make simple things sound complex to take your money.


STEP 4 — Be polite
Description: Treat everyone like an equal.
Tips:
- Mind basic manners on the street and at the table.
- Say please and thank you.
- Don't talk down to people.
- Don't be rude, even to people you'll never see again.


STEP 5 — Keep the mood light
Description: You don't need a stand-up routine. Just be playful.
Tips:
- Give unexpected or exaggerated answers to casual questions.
- Stay away from internet references. Most people aren't as online as you are.
- A little humour goes a long way.


END STATE
If you look presentable, smell nice, are mentally stable, treat people with respect,
and carry a bit of confidence and humour, you're already ahead of half the population.
Ready for the next phase.
