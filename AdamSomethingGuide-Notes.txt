# BEFORE YOU START

OVERVIEW
This is what awaits you:
- You meet people and eventually get interested in someone.
- The two of you go on dates to know more about each other.
- You fall in love and enter an exclusive relationship.
- You maintain that relationship and share your life for as long as it makes you both happy.

if you go into dating prepared, you’ll get much better results. If you know what your dealbreakers and red lines are, how to conduct yourself during and outside dates, and what to look for in others, you'll have a better time

Romantic feelings are conditional. Meaning, if you do things that turn the other person
off, they will like you less. If you do things that turn them on, they will like you more. on the spectrum between the strongest love up high, and complete
indifference at rock bottom, there is a small, thin line. It just sits there, right in the middle, at an
exactly equal distance from both extremes. This line can only be crossed one way: down. once the romantic spark is gone, it’s never coming back. the relationship – if you even made it that far – has too much negative
baggage, and can never be truly restarted

If someone won’t touch you because they only date people 2 meters tall or above, or
D-cup or above, or 160 cm or below, chances are you’d want nothing to do with them to begin
with. Think about it: if they’re so shallow they’d miss a great catch (i.e. you, hopefully) because
you’re a few centimeters off here or there, then good riddance. You’ve dodged a bullet.
personality traits such as sense of humor and confidence overwhelmingly
trump superficial things like height. Different people are into different things;

the basics
Present the best version of yourself
- Dress nice.
- Smell nice.
- Look too clean.
- Shave or trim any hair (head, face, body) to your taste.
- Cut and clean your nails.
- Wear simple, not worned out, good quality cloths that make you feel comfortable.


Personality, traits are universally attractive:
- Confidence.
- Good manners.
- Being somewhat challenging.
- At least some sense of humor.
TIP: Forget the charisma gurus. The make the simple sound complex so they can have your money and attention.


Question: “How do I become confident?”
Answer: “Fake it till you make it.”
Confidence means being the only one in the room who knows you’re insecure. Everyone
has insecurities, but confident people don’t put theirs on display.
TIP: don’t be an emotionless brick. Just don't advertise them. You can be vulnerable later.
Also, no self-deprecating humor, that will undermine your confidence aura.

Stay cool. Slow yourself down, don’t flail around, keep a relaxed posture. Straighten up and put
your shoulders back, and, above all, practice maintaining eye contact with people you interact
with.
TIP: If you’re shy and/or socially awkward by default, this will be difficult for the first five
hundred times, but it’ll get better, I promise.


Politeness.
This one is self-explanatory I hope. Don’t be rude. Be aware of basic manners, both on
the street and by the table. Say “please” and “thank you”. Don’t talk down to people. Treat
everyone with respect, like equals.


You don't need to do a stand up comedy act, or memorise jokes. Keep the mood light and playful.
- Give an unexpected or exaggerated answer to a casual question
- Stay away from internet references, not everyone spends as much time there as you
